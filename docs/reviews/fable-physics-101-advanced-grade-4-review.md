# Claude Fable review: Physics 101 Advanced Grade 4

Date: 21 July 2026  
Model: `claude-fable-5`  
Scope: bounded review of the proposed course sequence, misconception model, animated demonstrations and assessment blueprint. No secrets, profiles, credentials or unrelated files were included.

## Verdict

Fable judged the initial architecture strong but identified three material risks:

1. the original Push/Pull/Support chapter and Machines chapter were overloaded for 180 seconds;
2. the sequence omitted the high-value “force runs out” and “heavier falls faster” misconceptions;
3. fixed ten-question tests would allow answer memorisation across unlimited retakes.

## Changes applied

- Removed tension as a Chapter 3 teaching target; it may only be named as a rope pull during the capstone.
- Reduced Machines to ramps and levers under one causal idea: trade force for distance. Pulleys were deferred.
- Removed the formal mass/weight distinction from the gravity chapter.
- Added explicit stored-force reasoning to Chapter 2 and heavier-falls-faster reasoning to Chapter 8.
- Introduced one rubber-band force meter in Chapter 3 and reused it across course data examples.
- Reframed the inquiry chapter as **The Broken Experiment**, with a deliberately flawed friction test as its hero sequence.
- Specified a consistent course-wide arrow grammar with labelled object pairs and stable colours.
- Expanded each chapter to a 20–30 item bank while retaining ten questions per visible attempt.
- Changed the capstone into one linked rescue scenario; the video supplies evidence and failed designs, while the test contains the child’s engineering decisions.
- Added a mastery-oriented capstone gate for user calibration rather than silently hard-coding it.

## Judgement retained by Bright Quest

The plan does not copy Fable’s suggestion that friction arrows visually “grow” while a rolling ball stops. The clearer Grade 4 model is that the kick interaction ends when the foot leaves the ball, while friction continues to act between the ball and surface. This avoids replacing one stored-force misconception with an unstable arrow model.

## Non-negotiable gates carried forward

- One causal idea per visual beat.
- No more than three new terms per chapter.
- Every distractor maps to a named misconception or evidence error.
- Every misconception is named and disproved with visible evidence.
- Arrow grammar remains consistent across all chapters.
- Retakes rotate variants.
- No question relies on knowledge unavailable in the unlocked chapters.
- The capstone requires demonstrated prior engagement, not watch time alone.
