## User decision after review

The implementation plan retains the Fable safeguards that support the selected direction: reduced density, brighter spacing, physical evidence, tablet-safe type, stronger force patterns and a pacing/comprehension gate.

## Strongest decisions

- The fixed discipline of one hero action, one evidence model and no more than three labels.
- The reveal sequence: environment, object, motion, force, evidence, label.
- A small, repeatable transition vocabulary with an explicit banned list.
- Prerendered narration as the master clock.
- A separate voice audition using the same excerpt and timings.

## Risks identified

1. A complex graph squeezed into the 24% evidence lane may become unreadable on a 10-inch tablet.
2. Gravity violet and magnetism magenta need stronger colour/pattern separation.
3. Friction and air resistance need related but distinguishable orange treatments.
4. The rubber-band meter must be described as a comparison proxy, not a precise unit instrument.
5. A fixed composition needs a controlled variation vocabulary to avoid 11 chapters looking identical.
6. The proposed 150–165 words per minute is too fast for dense science language and simultaneous diagram reading.
7. The Studio's ornate title rail and floating glass evidence panel contradict the no-dashboard direction.
8. “Focus wash” needs a precise luminance-safe definition.
9. The 180-second pacing budget needs to be proven on a representative fair-test chapter.
10. A target-age comprehension check is required; visual quality cannot be judged by taste alone.

## Applied improvements

- Minimum master-frame label height: 40 px; diagram stroke: 4 px; label/evidence contrast: at least 4.5:1.
- Complex evidence receives a dedicated full-stage beat instead of being reduced into the side lane.
- Friction uses solid orange; air resistance uses dashed orange. Gravity becomes a deeper violet with long dashes; magnetism stays magenta with a dot-dash pattern.
- One canonical comparison-only rubber-band meter asset and wording are locked in the art bible.
- Voice target becomes 140–155 words per minute with deliberate silence before evidence and label reveals.
- Music is off by default; if a lesson earns it, it sits at least 12 dB below speech and ducks further around scientific terms.
- The variation vocabulary is limited to approved props, two camera framings, evidence type, surface and restrained time-of-day changes.
- The focus wash is defined as mild peripheral desaturation with no material luminance loss.
- Chapter 4 receives a 180-second pacing proof before visual direction lock.

## Recommended hard gates

1. Force lexicon, type scale and contrast spec approved.
2. Revised Chapter 4 keyframe passes greyscale, tablet-at-40-cm, caption-collision and three-label checks.
3. A small, consent-based target-age comprehension test confirms the learner can identify which surface produced the longer distance and explain why.
4. Voice is locked from measured auditions, not descriptions.
5. All 11 hero frames form one recognisable show when pinned side by side.
6. Every transition maps to the five approved motifs or is removed.

## Production implication

Do not treat any generated keyframe as ready-to-build artwork. After the user chooses a direction, create one revised final style frame that incorporates the approved Fable fixes, then run the visual/type/legibility gates before runtime implementation.
