## Recovery standard

- Show the physical event first, then reveal the evidence model.
- Give every narration cue at least two meaningful visual changes.
- Never exceed six seconds during active narration without object, evidence or focus movement.
- Keep the recurring Kinetic Workshop stage, same two skaters, cobalt/gold apparatus and warm bright lighting.
- Animate objects independently; background drift does not count as learning motion.
- Keep at most one action, one evidence model and three short labels in a frame.
- Use continuous transitions tied to cause and effect rather than fade-to-a-new-slide cuts.

## Motion grammar

1. Physical action: accelerate, collide, separate, roll, fall or stretch.
2. Evidence trace: motion trail, before/after ghost, distance ticks or speed dots.
3. Force model: a labelled arrow draws only after the interacting pair is named.
4. Focus: the irrelevant workshop area softens while the active objects stay bright.
5. Transition: the moving object carries into the next scene wherever possible.
