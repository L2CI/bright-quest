# Physics 101 Visual-Direction Review Packet

Date: 21 July 2026  
Scope: bounded preproduction design critique; no source code, secrets, personal data or unrelated files.

## Product

Bright Quest Physics 101 is an advanced Grade 4 course about forces, motion and evidence. It must match Chemistry 101's operational format: 11 chapters, about 180 seconds of prerecorded animation and teacher narration per chapter, optional captions, ten-question tests, selected-child progress and Parent Cockpit evidence.

The visual bar is being raised. The course should feel premium, bright, calm and scientifically exact—not like a dark blackboard, a card-heavy quiz app, a noisy game HUD or a generic slide deck.

## Fixed conceptual layout

- 1920 × 1080 master frame.
- Top 9%: short orientation rail.
- Centre-left 66% × 69%: one large observable demonstration.
- Right 24% × 69%: one quieter evidence model, graph, meter or comparison.
- Bottom 12%: protected caption-safe band.
- 5% safety on all edges and all essential objects within central 80%.
- One hero action, one evidence model and no more than three short labels per frame.
- Player controls live outside the lesson video and never cover diagrams.

## Shared art and teaching grammar

- Bright but restrained palette: sky blue, cyan, warm cream, gold, orange and small violet/magenta accents, anchored by midnight teal.
- Tangible materials and subtle dimensionality; teaching-critical diagrams remain deterministic and exact.
- Reveal environment, object, motion, force arrow, evidence, then label.
- Applied force/rope gold; support cyan; friction/air orange; gravity violet; magnetism magenta.
- No colour-only meaning: labels and line patterns reinforce force families.
- Recurring rubber-band meter provides a Grade 4-safe force proxy.

## Three generated directions

### Sunlit Discovery Studio

Airy near-future science studio, cream floor, cyan daylight, translucent evidence glass and a beautifully manufactured blue/gold cart. The sample has the clearest hierarchy and most optimistic brightness. Risks: the evidence panel may feel like a game/UI overlay; the title rail is more ornate than necessary; the large glass panel could consume too much attention.

### Kinetic Workshop

Angled hands-on workbench with warm timber, cream track, powder-coated blue equipment, built-in distance rule and a real force-meter apparatus. The sample has the strongest tactile measurement story and makes the investigation feel buildable. Risks: it is denser, less cinematic and closer to a static worksheet; repeated pegboard insets could become small at tablet size; the bottom caption-safe band needs firmer protection.

### Field Evidence Expedition

Sunlit school-garden test track with physical surface lanes, cinematic depth and a slim navy evidence ribbon. The sample has the strongest real-world context and story potential while staying bright. Risks: background detail can compete with the experiment; the evidence ribbon is dark and somewhat separate from the world; decorative top tracking marks add little teaching value.

All three depict the same Chapter 4 fair-test comparison: the same cart moves farther on a smooth surface and a shorter distance on a grippier textured surface.

## Voice and motion proposal

- Audition the same 35–45 second Chapter 1 excerpt in three voices before production: approved energetic `coral`; a deeper/calmer `coral`; and warm teacher-like `onyx`.
- Target 150–165 spoken words per minute, varied sentence endings, exact science diction and no cartoon, military or trailer cadence.
- Prerender TTS; stored MP3 is the master clock. Music, if used, is subtle and ducked under speech.
- Reuse five transition motifs only: purposeful draw, match-action cut, evidence settle, diagram morph and focus wash.
- Avoid spins, white flashes, zoom-through-text, persistent bouncing and narration stretches over eight seconds without meaningful visual change.
