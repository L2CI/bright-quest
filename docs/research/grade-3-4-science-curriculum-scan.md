# Grade 3/4 Science Curriculum Scan for Bright Quest Chemistry/Physics

Date: 2026-07-06  
Scope: Phase 2 research scan only. This is not yet the final curriculum map or storyboard plan.
Research timestamp: sources were checked on 2026-07-06. Curriculum pages can change; re-check exact document links before publishing a formal curriculum map.

## 1. Executive summary

Official Australian and Victorian curriculum sources strongly support a Grade 3/4 opening arc around heat transfer, solids and liquids, state change, water cycle processes, material properties, contact/non-contact forces, friction, gravity and magnets. UK and NGSS comparisons broaden this into sound, light, electricity, energy transfer and simple design challenges. Matter particles, dissolving, mixtures, separation and chemical change are mostly stronger as a carefully scaffolded extension: they are present in later primary standards internationally, so they can be introduced to an advanced 8-year-old through concrete observations and visual models, but not through formal atomic structure, equations or abstract chemistry.

Recommended synthesis direction: begin with observable materials and changes, then introduce particles only as a simple explanatory model after learners have handled real phenomena. Keep every chapter in the cycle "observe -> predict -> test -> explain", use teacher-led safety boundaries, and treat misconceptions explicitly. Physics should be framed as patterns of interaction and energy movement rather than formula work.

## 3. AU curriculum baseline

Curriculum facts from ACARA v9.0 Science Year 3/4:

- `AC9S3U03`: identify sources of heat energy and examine how temperature changes when heat energy is transferred from one object to another.
- `AC9S3U04`: investigate the observable properties of solids and liquids and how adding or removing heat energy leads to a change of state.
- `AC9S4U02`: identify sources of water and describe key processes in the water cycle, including movement of water through the sky, landscape and ocean; precipitation; evaporation; and condensation.
- `AC9S4U03`: identify how forces can be exerted by one object on another and investigate the effect of frictional, gravitational and magnetic forces on the motion of objects.
- `AC9S4U04`: examine the properties of natural and made materials including fibres, metals, glass and plastics and consider how these properties influence their use.
- Science Inquiry in both Years 3 and 4 expects students to pose questions, use scaffolds to plan fair investigations, make formal measurements, use tables/graphs/models, compare findings, draw conclusions and communicate using scientific vocabulary. Map each individual inquiry descriptor before final curriculum claims rather than using a range shorthand.

Relevant ACARA elaboration implications:

- Heat should be sensed, measured with thermometers and modelled as movement from warmer to cooler objects.
- State change should use safe examples such as ice, sealed-bag freezing/melting, butter or chocolate.
- Materials should be compared by observed properties and linked to use.
- Friction, gravity and magnets should be investigated through real objects and visual force arrows.
- Safety rules are an explicit inquiry expectation, including careful handling of equipment and materials.

Curriculum synthesis from the AU baseline:

- The core Grade 3/4 chemistry sequence should not begin with atoms. It should begin with materials, properties, solids/liquids, heat and observable change.
- Particles can be used as an explanatory model after observable state change, but should be introduced as "tiny bits in a model" rather than formal atoms, ions, molecules or electron structure.
- Grade 3/4 physics can include pushes, pulls, friction, gravity, magnets, heat, sound, light and simple electricity if kept concrete and qualitative.

## 4. International comparison

England lower Key Stage 2 aligns closely with the proposed Bright Quest scope:

- Year 3: light, shadows, forces and magnets.
- Year 4: states of matter, sound and electricity.
- Year 4 state-change guidance includes solids/liquids/gases, heating/cooling, evaporation and condensation, and cautions against heating examples that involve chemical change.

NGSS gives a useful progression boundary:

- Grade 3 focuses on forces, motion patterns and magnetic/electric interactions.
- Grade 4 focuses on energy transfer by sound, light, heat and electric currents.
- Matter particles, dissolving, evaporating salt water, material identification and new substances are Grade 5 physical science expectations. For Bright Quest, that means these can be advanced extension ideas, but they need extra concrete scaffolding and misconception checks.

Victoria confirms the AU path:

- Levels 3/4 include heat, solid/liquid state change, material properties and direct/distance forces.
- The Level 4 achievement standard expects heat/temperature distinction, heat transfer examples, solid/liquid state-change explanations, material-use reasoning and contact/non-contact force descriptions.

NSW is useful for lesson architecture:

- The 2024 syllabus emphasises focus areas, practices, vocabulary and written explanation.
- It supports making each chapter an inquiry story rather than a topic lecture.

## 5. Recommended scope for an advanced Grade 3/4 learner

Recommended app-core for the first matter arc:

- Materials and observable properties: flexible/rigid, transparent/opaque, absorbent/waterproof, magnetic/non-magnetic, thermal conductor/insulator at a simple observational level. Traceability: AU `AC9S4U04`; VIC `VCSSU060`.
- Solids and liquids first, then gases through air, bubbles, water vapour visuals and inflated containers. Traceability: AU `AC9S3U04`; England Year 4 states of matter; NGSS Grade 5 extension for particles.
- Heating and cooling: heat sources, heat transfer, temperature measurement, melting, freezing, evaporation and condensation. Traceability: AU `AC9S3U03`, `AC9S3U04`, `AC9S4U02`; VIC `VCSSU059`, `VCSSU063`.
- Particle model as a visual explanation after observable phenomena. Traceability: extension from NGSS 5.Structure and Properties of Matter; use only as a model for Grade 3/4.
- Melting vs disappearing and dissolving vs melting. Traceability: AU state-change baseline plus NGSS Grade 5 dissolving extension.

Recommended later physics batch:

- Forces: push, pull, friction, gravity, magnets, contact vs non-contact forces, motion patterns and fair tests. Traceability: AU `AC9S4U03`; VIC `VCSSU064`; NGSS Grade 3 forces; England Year 3 forces and magnets.
- Energy transfer: heat, sound, light and electric current as ways energy moves. Traceability: AU heat `AC9S3U03`; NGSS Grade 4 energy; England Year 3 light and Year 4 sound/electricity.
- Simple circuits: complete loop, cell/battery, wires, bulb, buzzer, switch, conductor/insulator, safety with low voltage kits only. Traceability: NGSS Grade 4 energy; England Year 4 electricity.
- Sound as vibration and simple wave-pattern modelling. Traceability: England Year 4 sound; NGSS Grade 4 energy.
- Light as travel/reflection/shadows, without formal ray diagrams beyond simple arrows. Traceability: England Year 3 light; NGSS Grade 4 energy.

Use as stretch/extension:

- Particle model for states of matter, gases and dissolving, always labelled as a model.
- Conservation idea at a qualitative level: "the stuff has not vanished", not equation balancing.
- Chemical vs physical change through safe observations and evidence clues.
- Field idea for magnets as "space around a magnet where its pull can act", not mathematical field strength.

Demonstration context tags:

- App-core: safe to show directly in animation or interactive simulation.
- Home optional: can be done with household materials, parent-supervised, no heat beyond warm tap water.
- Adult demonstration only: may be shown by a parent/teacher, but not as a child-led task.
- Defer home: keep as app-only or omit until a safer setup exists.

## 6. Topics to include now vs defer

Include now:

- States of matter as behaviour and observable properties.
- Melting/freezing/evaporation/condensation.
- Dissolving as spreading through a liquid, with sugar/salt examples.
- Mixtures and simple separation, with filtering/sieving as home optional and evaporation as app-only or adult demonstration only.
- Friction, gravity and magnets, preferably in a later physics batch after the matter arc.
- Sound vibration, light/shadows and simple circuits, preferably in a later physics batch after the matter arc.
- Fair testing, measurement, models, tables and explanation from evidence.

Defer or treat only as a teaser:

- Atomic structure, protons/neutrons/electrons, electron shells and bonding.
- Periodic table memorisation.
- Formal molecules, ions, valency or chemical formulae beyond familiar names.
- Balanced chemical equations and quantitative conservation of mass.
- Acids/alkalis beyond safe indicator demonstrations led by an adult.
- Combustion experiments, flames, strong acids/bases, oxidisers or unknown chemicals.
- Density calculations, pressure laws, voltage/current formal definitions, resistance formulae.
- Wave frequency, amplitude equations and electromagnetic spectrum detail.
- Energy conservation as a formal law.

## 7. Safety considerations for experiments

Minimum safety rules for Bright Quest chapters:

- All home demonstrations are optional, parent-supervised and use common household materials.
- No flames, boiling water, hot oil, strong chemicals, glass heating, inhalation, tasting unknowns, pressure build-up or closed-container heating.
- Use ice, warm tap water, sealed bags, chocolate/butter softened by warm hands or warm water nearby, salt/sugar water, paper towels, sieves, coffee filters, large magnets, toy cars, cardboard ramps, rubber bands, torches and low voltage circuit kits.
- Avoid asking a child to identify substances by smell or taste.
- Use batteries only in age-appropriate holders. No mains electricity, damaged batteries, loose coin cells or deliberate short circuits.
- Magnets should be large enough not to be swallowed and kept away from small children, pacemakers, electronics and medical devices. Do not use loose button magnets.
- Steam/water vapour, boiling, hot kettles and evaporation of salt water should be app-only or adult demonstration only. Do not ask a child to handle hot water or steam.
- Any "cloud in a bottle", indicator or circuit demonstration should be teacher/parent-led. Avoid pressure build-up and aerosols unless the setup is explicitly approved later.
- The app should present safety notes in plain language before optional demonstrations.
